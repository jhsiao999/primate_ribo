Goal

We investigate protein divergence relative to RNA divergence and consider if attenuation at the protein level is more likely to be associated with attenuation at the RNA level, and if enhancement at the protein level is more likely to be associated with enhancement at the RNA level.

Definitions

Of all the 3,188 genes included in the analysis, we apply divergence definition version-20150814 and investigate genes that fall into the following three categories

  1. attenuated at the protein level relative to the RNA level
    • Sig. different between RNA and protein divergence) AND Sig. RNA DE AND ( (the absolute value of protein divergence is smaller than the absolute value of RNA divergence) OR (protein divergence is in the opposite direction of RNA divergence) )
  2. enhanced at the protein level relative to the RNA level
    • Sig. different between RNA and protein divergence) AND Sig. RNA DE AND ( (the absolute value of protein divergence is greater than the absolute value of RNA divergence) AND (protein divergence is in the opposite direction of RNA divergence) )
  3. null divergence: of the 3,188 genes, those that do not fall in the above two categories.

eSet.temp <- eSetRRP.log2[ ,eSetRRP.log2$species!="rhesus"]

fc.mat <- lapply( seq_along( c("human","chimp") ), function(i) {
  ii <- eSet.temp$species == c("human","chimp")[i]
  eSet.tt <- eSet.temp[, ii]
  emat <- lapply( seq_along( c("ribo","rna","protein") ), function(j) {
    jj <- eSet.tt$seqData == c("ribo","rna","protein")[j]
    rowMeans( exprs(eSet.tt[, jj] ), na.rm=TRUE )
  })
  emat <- do.call(cbind, emat)
  colnames(emat) <- c("ribo","rna","protein")
  return( data.frame(emat) )
})
names(fc.mat) <- c("human","chimp")

dmat_unnormed <- data.frame( ribo = fc.mat$human$ribo-fc.mat$chimp$ribo,

RNA vs. Ribo divergence

## Genes with sig. human/chimp difference in divergence between RNA and Ribo
ii_sig_rnaribo <- res.riborna$int.qval < .01

## Genes with sig. human/chimp difference in RNA levels
ii_sig_RNA_DE <- rnaRes.Q$qval < .01

## Genes with sig. human/chimp difference in Ribo levels
ii_sig_Ribo_DE <- riboRes.Q$qval < .01


## Category 1 - Enhanced divergence, criteria are as follows
## a) Sig. RNA vs. Ribo difference, AND
## b) Sig. DE at RNA level, AND
## c) ( abs(RNA FC) < abs(Ribo FC) ) AND ( RNA FC * Ribo FC > 0)
ii_riboGTrna <- (ii_sig_rnaribo) & ii_sig_RNA_DE & 
                ( (abs(xy.riborna$ribo) > abs(xy.riborna$rna)) & 
                  (xy.riborna$rna * xy.riborna$ribo > 0 ) )


## Category 2 - Attenuated divergence, criteria are as follows
## a) Sig. RNA vs. Ribo diff., AND
## b) Sig. DE at RNA level, AND 
## c) ( abs(RNA FC) > abs(Ribo FC) ) AND ( RNA FC * Ribo FC < 0)
ii_rnaGTribo <- (ii_sig_rnaribo) & ii_sig_RNA_DE & 
                ( (abs(xy.riborna$rna) > abs(xy.riborna$ribo) ) | 
                  (xy.riborna$rna * xy.riborna$ribo < 0) ) 


## Category 3, Pseudo enhacement, criteria are as follows
## a) Sig. RNA vs. Ribo diff., AND
## b) No sig. DE at RNA level, AND 
## c) Sig. DE at Ribo level
ii_null_rnaGTribo <- ii_sig_rnaribo & !ii_sig_RNA_DE & ii_sig_Ribo_DE


## Category 4, Null divergence, criteria are as follows
## a) Sig. RNA vs. Ribo diff., AND
## b) No sig. DE at RNA level, AND 
## c) No sig. DE at Ribo level
ii_null_rnaNULLribo <- ii_sig_rnaribo & !ii_sig_RNA_DE & !ii_sig_Ribo_DE


Ribo_refmat <- rbind.data.frame(attenuated = sum(ii_rnaGTribo),

Signal in RNA ~ Protein

Among the 408 genes with RNA and Ribo significant differences and no significant difference between RNA and protein, we do a follow-up analysis to look further to consider the contribution/relation of translation divergence to protein divergence, relative to RNA divergence.

Yoav: So, for example, if gene 1 (among the 408 genes with sig. diff. between RNA and Ribo) shows attenuated translation relative to RNA, protein divergence will be attenuated relative to RNA divergence. If gene 2 (among the 408 genes with sig. diff. between RNA and Ribo) shows enhanced ribo divergence compared with RNA, I predict that the protein divergence will also be enhanced compared with RNA.

Attenuated translation

Among the 62 genes that were attenuated at the translation level relative to the transcript level (RNA-Ribo attenuation), we tested the null hypothesis that these genes are as likely to be attenuated as to be enhanced at the protein level relative to the transcript level (RNA-Protein). If this null hypothesis were rejected and the number of genes that were attenuated at the protein layer is larger than the number of genes that were enhanced at the protein layer, then we say that attenuation at the translation level is more likey to co-occur with attenuation at the protein level than with enhancement at the protein level.

RNA > Protein

Among the genes that are attenuated at the protein level compared to the RNA level (RNA > protein), we are interested in the number of genes with attenuated translation. In other words, we ask whether genes that were attenuated at the protein level were more likely to be enriched with genes attenuated at the translation level.

For the hypothesis testing, we computed number of genes enriched in translation attenuation among the genes enriched for protein attenuation in the set of 2999 with similar divergence levels between RNA and protein.
